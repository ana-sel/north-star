The weird words, in plain English

You don't need to be technical to read this page. Here's every unusual word translated into everyday language — once you know these six, the rest of the page is easy.

📱 AAB / APK Android app files

APK is an Android app package for direct install/testing. AAB (Android App Bundle) is the format Google Play expects for store upload. In this plan, you build these from Expo, test on your phone, then publish to the store.

🗄️ Postgres Database

A database is just a very organised digital filing cabinet. Postgres is a popular, reliable, free one. It's where your nights of sleep are stored as neat rows — like a spreadsheet the app reads and writes.

🔒 RLS Privacy lock

Short for Row-Level Security. A "row" is one entry (one night). This is a rule the database enforces itself: you can only ever see rows that belong to you. Even if a friend uses the same app, the database refuses to show them your data.

🎟️ JWT Login pass

Short for JSON Web Token — think of it as a wristband you get after signing in. The app shows this wristband with every request so the system knows it's really you, without asking for your password again.

☁️ Supabase The cloud

A free service that bundles the three things above — login, the database, and an automatic way to read/write it — into one place online that's always switched on. It's the single platform you'd look after.

⚙️ Edge Function Tiny helper

A small piece of code that runs in the cloud only when called. Ours has one job: take your week's numbers, ask the AI for a gentle note, and send the note back. It also hides the AI key so it's never on your phone.

00 — The decision

Simpler than before: two places, not three

The earlier plan ran the AI on your laptop with a public tunnel. That works, but it's the hardest part to keep alive — the laptop must always be on. The simpler design moves the AI into the cloud as a tiny function that calls a free hosted model. One platform to look after, nothing running at home.

  • Phone (app)
  • Cloud (database + login)
  • Laptop + tunnel running Ollama
  • ⚠ Laptop must stay on
  • ⚠ Tunnel to configure & maintain
  • ⚠ Two deploy targets
→

After — 2 places

  • Cloud (database + login + AI function)
  • ✓ Nothing runs at home
  • ✓ One platform: Supabase
  • ✓ AI key hidden server-side

02 — How the pieces talk

Every conversation, step by step

There are only three conversations the app ever has. All of them go over HTTPS and carry your login token so the cloud knows it's you.

A Signing in

Phone→"Sign in with Google" → Supabase Auth
Supabase Auth→verifies with Google, returns a login token (JWT)
Phone✓stores the token; now every request proves who you are

B Saving & reading sleep

Phone→insert / select on sleep_entries via supabase-js (token attached)
Database→row-level security checks the token: only your rows pass
Database✓returns your nights → phone draws the chart

C Getting the AI note

Phone→calls Edge Function sleep-note with the week's numbers
Edge Function→prompts a free hosted model (key stays server-side)
Edge Function✓returns 2–3 calm sentences · or a rule-based note if the model is unavailable
03 — Where the database is & how the phone uses it

One cloud database, locked per person

The database is a hosted Postgres inside Supabase — not on the phone, not on your laptop. The phone never connects to it directly; it goes through the auto API, and row-level security guarantees each person sees only their own data.

03b — Your privacy (please read)

Will the AI share my data on websites?

Short answer: no — the AI does not post your data anywhere, and no human browses it. But it's important you understand the one moment your numbers do leave, so you can choose how private you want it to be.

The honest truth: to write your gentle note, the cloud helper sends your sleep/energy/mood numbers to an AI company (like Google) over a private, encrypted connection. It sends numbers only — never your name, email, or login. The AI reads them, writes 2–3 sentences, and that's it. It is not published, posted, or shown to anyone. The only caveat: some free AI plans may use what they receive to improve their models — so below you get to choose.

Everything on the left never leaves your locked database. Only the numbers on the right travel — and only to write your note.
Mode 1 · Simplest

Cloud AI note

Best if you want zero setup and don't mind numbers being read by a big AI company.

  • ✓Nothing to run at home — the cloud does it all.
  • ✓Only numbers leave; no name, email, or login.
  • !A free AI plan may use those numbers to train its model.
  • ✓Never posted publicly; no person reads it.
Mode 2 · Most private

On-device / your-machine AI

Best if you want nothing at all to leave — matches Compass's privacy promise.

  • ✓The AI runs on your own computer (Ollama). Numbers never leave.
  • ✓No AI company ever sees anything.
  • !Needs your computer switched on to write the note.
  • ✓If it's off, a built-in plain note appears — still no data leaves.
You can switch modes anytime — it's just a setting. Start with Mode 1 to get going quickly, and move to Mode 2 later if you'd rather keep everything on your own machine. Either way, your data is never sold, never posted, and never shown to another person.
