# Compass — design philosophy & psychological safety

> The constitution. Every screen, path, quality, word, and interaction in Compass
> must obey this document. If a feature conflicts with a principle here, the
> principle wins. We are changing how a person relates to themselves — that is
> sacred and dangerous. First, do no harm.

---

## 0. North Star — what Compass *is* and *is not*

Compass is a **compass, not a map.** It does not hand you a destination or a
person to become. It helps you find *your own* north — who you already are, the
life you've actually built, and the self and future you genuinely want — and it
keeps you oriented as you walk there.

| Compass IS | Compass is NOT |
|---|---|
| A mirror that helps you see yourself clearly | A mould that presses you into an ideal |
| A gentle mentor / guide | A coach who shames, drills, or ranks you |
| A way to **unfold** your authentic self | A way to **perform** a better-looking self |
| A steadier relationship *with yourself* | Another source of "not enough" |
| Something you need **less** over time | Something engineered to keep you hooked |

The goal (Kegan's arc): move toward a **self-authoring** life — you know who you
are, you chose it, and you can hold your own compass. Success = the person needs
Compass less, not more.

---

## 1. First, do no harm — the non-negotiables

These are hard limits. No feature may violate them.

1. **Never manufacture a False Self.** (Winnicott, Rogers.) We build *from* who
   the person already is — their lived strengths and values — before any growth.
   Growth is framed as *unfolding latent capacity*, never *fixing a deficient person*.
2. **Worth is never conditional.** Progress, streaks, and "levels" never imply the
   person is more or less worthy, ahead or behind, whole or broken.
3. **Regulation before insight.** (Polyvagal / Siegel's window of tolerance.) Never
   push someone into deep emotional or trauma material. Stabilise, resource, ground
   first. The app is not a therapist and must say so.
4. **No rumination or comparison loops.** No hoardable lists of what you lack, no
   leaderboards, no social comparison, no fixation objects. (Anti-limerence,
   anti-Nolen-Hoeksema rumination.)
5. **Rupture is met with repair, never shame.** A missed day, a broken streak, a
   "no" — answered with warmth. (Self-compassion: Neff.)
6. **Autonomy is absolute.** Compass suggests; the person decides. No "you must,"
   no manufactured urgency, no dark patterns. (Self-Determination Theory.)
7. **Integration, not fragmentation.** Parts work moves toward wholeness and
   Self-leadership (IFS), never toward dissociation or self-splitting.
8. **Know your edges.** On signs of crisis (self-harm, abuse, severe distress),
   Compass steps back and points to real human help. It never plays clinician.

## 2. The ten design principles (with their roots)

Each principle names its source(s) and the concrete rule it imposes.

1. **Start from strengths, not deficits.**
   *Positive psychology (VIA strengths, Seligman/Peterson).* Before offering
   anything to grow, reflect back what's already true and good. The first
   experience of Identity is recognition, not a to-do list of flaws.

2. **Motivation must be autonomous, not introjected.**
   *SDT (Deci & Ryan).* Every practice ties to the person's *own* values and
   reasons ("your why"), never to external "shoulds." We evoke, we don't impose
   (Motivational Interviewing). If it becomes an obligation, it's failing.

3. **Every quality has a mean — track excess, not just lack.**
   *Aristotle's golden mean; Delphic "nothing in excess."* Courage sits between
   cowardice and recklessness; warmth between coldness and enmeshment; boundaries
   between walls and doormat. Compass watches for **over**use (rigidity,
   distortion) as much as underuse. This is a primary guard against turning a
   person into a caricature of one trait.

4. **Grow by unfolding, not forcing.**
   *Taoism — wu wei, the uncarved block (p'u).* We help the person's nature
   emerge; we do not carve them into a shape. Rest, seasons, and *not* growing are
   legitimate. Permission to pause is always visible.

5. **Admiration is a mirror.**
   *Jungian projection; Rumi.* What you're drawn to in others is evidence of a
   seed in you. The app always turns longing inward into grounded practice — never
   outward into fixation or comparison.

6. **Self-compassion is the operating temperature.**
   *Neff; Gilbert's compassion-focused therapy.* The voice is warm, never a critic.
   Failure is normal and met kindly. We install a kinder inner voice, not a louder
   critic.

7. **The body leads; ground before you reflect.**
   *Polyvagal (Porges); somatic work.* Reflection happens from a regulated,
   present, embodied place — not spiralling in the head. Every deep prompt is
   paired with a way back to the body and the present.

8. **Grow in connection, not isolation.**
   *Ubuntu — "I am because we are"; attachment theory.* Self-work includes
   relationships, repair, service, and belonging. We never sell lonely
   self-optimisation.

9. **Truth over flattery, kindness over bluntness.**
   *Rogerian congruence.* Mirrors are honest but gentle. No fake praise; no cruel
   candour. The person's own felt "yes" decides what's true of them (see §4).

10. **Meaning is the engine, behaviour is downstream.**
    *Frankl (logotherapy); ACT (values as directions, not destinations); Stoic
    virtue-as-the-good.* We orient to *why* and to values-as-direction. A compass
    points a way; it never declares you've "arrived."

11. **Few things, deeply — the Middle Way.**
    *Buddhist Middle Way & gradual-but-simultaneous cultivation; Aristotle's* hexis
    *(virtue as repeated practice); Epictetus — “no one makes progress wavering
    between many things”; habit science (a habit takes ~2 months to automate and
    multiplies badly in parallel — Lally 2010; Fogg; Clear); Taoist non-forcing.*
    Change happens through a **few** practices, repeated, over time. Not everything
    at once (overwhelm — nothing integrates), and **not** one-thing-perfected-
    before-any-other (rigid, stuck). **Foundations are never “finished”** — they
    deepen for life; you carry them forward while adding the next. The app holds a
    small, **shared focus budget** (spec-you §1.2) so Today never overwhelms, and
    adds gentle friction — never a wall — before you take on more.

## 3. The voice of Compass

- Warm, plain, unhurried. A wise, slightly older friend — never a guru, coach,
  drill sergeant, or therapist.
- Speaks *with* you, never *at* you. Invites, never instructs.
- Names hard things gently and honestly. Uses "you might," "when you're ready,"
  "there's no rush."
- Celebrates quietly. No confetti-spam, no manipulative praise, no guilt.
- On a missed day: *"Welcome back. Nothing's lost."* Never *"You broke your streak."*

---

## 4. Self-honesty over badges (how anything is "earned")

The app never awards you an identity. **You confirm it.**
- On completion of a practice/path: *"Does this feel like you now?"* →
  **Yes, part of me** / **Not yet — still growing** / **I'm not sure.**
- **"No / Not yet"**: no loss, no penalty. *"Good — honesty is the practice."*
  It keeps quietly going and eases the tracking pressure. Optionally: *"what will
  it look like when it's true?"* (one line).
- **"Not sure"**: extend the window; lean to the person's pace.
- Earned qualities are **permanent and un-deletable** *because the person claimed
  them as true* — not because the app decided.

---

## 5. Journeys, Camps & "levels" — the safe model

We want structure and milestones without rank or shame.

- **Not a ladder. A rite of passage.** (Van Gennep / Turner: separation →
  liminal → return-with-a-gift; Campbell's journey.) A Path is an *initiation*,
  not a level-up. You leave the familiar, cross a threshold, and return changed —
  bringing a gift (a quality) back to your life.
- **Camps = milestones** along a Path (already in the model). Each camp names the
  shift it produces and *builds in rest*.
- **No global "level" or score.** Nobody is "Level 2" while someone else is "Level
  9." Growth is shown as **terrain and seasons**, not rank. You can always see you
  are *somewhere on your own path*, never *behind on a shared one*.
- **Developmental direction, unlabelled.** We quietly orient toward Kegan's
  self-authoring maturity, but we never tell a person they're "less developed."

## 6. The Foundations — what every adult deserves to know

Almost no one was taught these. Compass offers them as **Foundations** anyone can
walk — not a test you pass/fail, a set of basic human tools. (Erikson's adult
tasks; life-skills research.) These are the "minimum kit for being a person":

1. **Regulate** — settle your own nervous system (breath, grounding, orienting).
2. **Feel & name** — an emotional vocabulary; feelings as information.
3. **Self-relationship** — self-parenting, the inner child, a kinder inner voice.
4. **Communicate** — needs, boundaries, honest asks (NVC), listening, repair.
5. **Relate** — attachment, conflict, intimacy, consent, trust.
6. **Know yourself** — values, needs, triggers, what's yours vs. inherited.
7. **Tend the body** — sleep, movement, food, rest as non-negotiable.
8. **Handle hard things** — grief, failure, uncertainty, endings.
9. **Live practically** — money, decisions, admin, planning a real life.
10. **Make meaning** — purpose, service, beauty, awe, the *why*.

The Foundations are the floor. Paths and Qualities are how you go beyond it — by
choice, from strength, at your pace.

---

## 7. Edges & referral — what Compass must NOT do

- Not diagnose. Not treat mental illness. Not do unsupervised trauma processing.
- Not replace therapy, medical care, or human relationship.
- On crisis signals: gently pause the growth machinery, express care, and surface
  **real human resources** (crisis lines, professionals, trusted people). Then get
  out of the way.
- Not create dependency. Periodically remind the person of their own agency and
  celebrate when they need the app less.

---

## 8. How this governs the specs

- `spec-you.md` and all later specs must cite these principles where relevant.
- Any list, prompt, streak, badge, or nudge is checked against §1 and §2 before it
  ships.
- Copy is checked against §3 (the voice).
- When in doubt: choose the gentler, more autonomy-respecting, more grounding
  option. Choose the person's authentic self over a more impressive one.
